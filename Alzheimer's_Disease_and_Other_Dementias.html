Alzheimer's Disease and Other Dementias

Alzheimer's Disease and other dementias are progressive neurological disorders that impair memory, thinking, and behavior, severely affecting daily life. Alzheimer's, the most common type of dementia, is characterized by the buildup of amyloid plaques and tau tangles in the brain. Symptoms include memory loss, confusion, difficulty with language and problem-solving, and changes in mood and personality. Other forms of dementia, such as vascular dementia and Lewy body dementia, also lead to cognitive decline but may have different underlying causes and symptom patterns. While there is currently no cure for Alzheimer's and most other dementias, early diagnosis and comprehensive care can help manage symptoms, improve quality of life, and support affected individuals and their families.
